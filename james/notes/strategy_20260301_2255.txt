# Market Research: High-Performing Note Articles (Simulated Analysis)

## 1. Top Performers (100+ Skis)
- **Characteristics:**
    - **Specific Numbers in Titles:** "Annual income increased by 2M yen" or "Saved 150 hours/year."
    - **"Tool" as a means, not an end:** They don't just review the tool; they review the *lifestyle change* caused by the tool.
    - **Contrarian View:** "Why I stopped using notion for X" or "Don't buy an iPad if you want to be productive."
    - **Targeting Pain Points:** Remote work fatigue, "imposter syndrome" in tech, inefficient meetings.

## 2. Low Performers (Self-Indulgent Diaries)
- **Characteristics:**
    - **Vague Titles:** "My thoughts on AI."
    - **No Actionable Advice:** Just complaining about work without a solution.
    - **Pure Specs:** Listing CPU speeds without context of *business value*.
