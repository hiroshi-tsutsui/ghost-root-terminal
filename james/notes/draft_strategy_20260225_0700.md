# James Note Strategy Draft (2026-02-25)

## Target Audience
- Job-hunting students (Shu-katsu sei) feeling desperate or mediocre.
- Young engineers realizing "coding alone won't make me rich".
- Anyone disgusted by the Japanese "recruit suit" homogeneity.

## Core Message (The "Contrarian" Hook)
"Stop doing 'Shu-katsu'. While you're memorizing fake ES (Entry Sheets), I was building things that made money. The US tech route doesn't care about your potential; it cares about your 'Proof of Work'."

## Tone & Style
- **Kansai Dialect**: Fast, cutting, slightly arrogant but deeply helpful.
- **Keywords**: "ROI", "Leverage", "Proof of Work", "GitHub > ES", "Async Communication".
- **Actionable Tool**: Notion (for portfolio), GitHub (for code), CleanShot X (for documenting UI/UX logic).

## Title Brainstorming
1. 【年収1000万】日本の「就活」なんか辞めとけ。米国AI企業が欲しがるのは「ES」じゃなくて「動くゴミ」や。
2. Fラン/文系でも米国テック企業に入れた理由。「面接対策」より「GitHubの草」を生やせ。

-> Selection: **Option 1** is strongest. It attacks the current pain point (ES hell) and offers a tangible alternative.

## Structure Outline
1. **Intro**: "Everyone looks the same in March. It's creepy. You're playing a losing game."
2. **The Trap**: Japanese companies hire for "potential" (compliance). US companies hire for "output" (immediate profit).
3. **My Story**: I wasn't a genius coder. I was a "Product Wrapper". I built ugly things that solved problems.
4. **The "Backdoor" (Strategy)**:
    - Don't polish your resume; polish your `README.md`.
    - Don't practice interviews; practice "Async Demos" (Loom/CleanShot).
    - The concept of "Proof of Work".
5. **Conclusion**: "Be the outlier. The scary path is actually the least crowded."
