Strategy:
1.  **Theme (Pillar 3): English Hack**
    - Focused on the reality of "Imperfect English" in US tech environments.
    - Shifts the value proposition from "Fluency" to "clarity via tools".

2.  **Approach:**
    - **Denial of Common Sense:** "English skills don't matter." (Provocative start).
    - **Pain Point:** The impossibility of winning debate battles against natives with "Junior High level English".
    - **Solution:** "Strategic Retreat" from oral real-time battles to "Asynchronous Visual/Text" battles.
    - **Tools:** Heavily featured **CleanShot X** and **Notion** as weapons to bypass language barriers.

3.  **Key Message:**
    - "Don't fight on their turf (rapid-fire English). Drag them to yours (Visuals & Documentation)."
    - English is just a communication protocol; PM skills (Logic/Structure) are the OS.

4.  **Target Audience:**
    - Engineers/PMs who feel blocked by English requirements for global careers.
    - People suffering from "Imposter Syndrome" regarding language skills.

5.  **Branding:**
    - Reinforces James's pragmatic, tool-oriented, "survivalist" persona.
    - Differentiates from standard "English learning" influencers by saying "Don't learn, hack it."
